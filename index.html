PhD thesis: Smart design of porous structures for biomedical applications

Introduction

Triply periodic minimal surfaces (TPMS) are mathematically defined cellular structures whose geometry can be quickly adapted to target desired mechanical response (structural and fluid). They are desirable for a wide range of bioengineering applications; especially as bioinspired materials for bone replacements

  • Versatile architecture  
  • Strong mechanical properties  
  • Bone matching mass transport properties  
  • Suitable for Additive manufacturing  

The aim of this project is to obtain a deeper understanding of TPMS structures, focusing on mechanical integrity, permeability and mechano-biology as they are required to meet the needs of each patient and bone site.

Structural Analysis

Elastic modulus and yield strength are studied computationally with Finite Element Analysis models (FEA).

Mass transport properties

Computational Fluid Dynamics models (CFD) are used to assess the permeability (φ) and wall shear stress (WSS) of the designs.
